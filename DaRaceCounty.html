Understanding the Vote Distribution

The data indicates a competitive race, with each district showing varied support for the candidates. The distribution of votes across districts highlights different levels of candidate preference, which can provide insights into regional support trends and voter turnout.

Comparing county-wide results to the City of Portland's results, Nathan Vasquez generally leads in both contexts. However, the vote distribution in the city shows a more pronounced lead for Vasquez, especially in District 4, suggesting stronger urban support. This highlights potential differences in voting patterns between urban and broader county regions.

Methods

This section describes the data processing and visualization methods used in this analysis.

Data Processing

The data normalization process was critical in ensuring fair comparison across different precincts. By normalizing the vote counts, we were able to highlight the relative performance of candidates in smaller versus larger precincts. This process involved adjusting vote counts to a common scale, which mitigated the effect of varying precinct sizes. Transparency in the visualization was used to represent this normalized data, where higher transparency indicated lower normalized values, and vice versa. This approach provided a clearer visual distinction between precincts with varying levels of voter support.

Data Visualization

The map visualization employs a color gradient to depict the percentage of votes for Nathan Vasquez across different precincts. The colors range from light purple to black, where lighter shades represent lower percentages and darker shades represent higher percentages. Transparency is also used to indicate the normalized vote counts, with lower transparency for smaller precincts and higher transparency for larger ones.

The following colors and districts are highlighted on the map:

  • Multnomah Co Commissioner District 1: Light Coral
  • Multnomah Co Commissioner District 2: Medium Sea Green
  • Multnomah Co Commissioner District 3: Cornflower Blue
  • Multnomah Co Commissioner District 4: Medium Orchid

To read the dashboard, look at the color intensity and transparency of each precinct. Darker colors indicate higher support for Nathan Vasquez, while lighter colors indicate lower support. The transparency level helps you understand the relative size of the precinct in terms of normalized vote counts. District boundaries are outlined in different colors for easy identification.

Interactive features include:

  • Hovering over precincts to change the cursor to a pointer, indicating interactivity.
  • A button to toggle between the full map and only the City of Portland, allowing users to focus on urban voting patterns.

This comprehensive visualization provides a clear and interactive way to understand the election results, highlighting both the geographical and numerical differences in voter support across Multnomah County.

How the Map Was Made

The interactive map of the Vasquez vs Schmidt 2024 race was created using several tools and data sources to provide an accurate view of the electoral landscape across Multnomah County.

  • Mapping Library: The map is built using MapLibre, an open-source mapping library that allows for the creation of high-quality, interactive maps.
  • Basemap: The basemap used is from OpenStreetMap, a community-driven and detailed geographic data provider.
  • Data Sources: The election results data was sourced from Multnomah County. The precinct-level results were joined with GeoJSON boundary data to visualize the voting patterns across the county effectively.

The process of creating this map involved integrating data from different sources, cleaning and transforming it to be suitable for mapping, and then visualizing the data in an interactive format to help users understand the voting distribution across Multnomah County and the City of Portland.
