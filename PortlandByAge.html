Portland Age Demographic Distribution: Data Overview

Welcome to our detailed analysis of Portland's age demographic distribution. This overview focuses solely on the data, providing insights into how different age groups are spread across the city's neighborhoods.

Census Tracts by Age Range

Our data reveals significant variations in the median age across different census tracts within Portland:

  • 18-30 years: 3 tracts
  • 31-40 years: 120 tracts
  • 41-50 years: 44 tracts
  • 51-60 years: 4 tracts
  • 61+ years: 0 tracts

The majority of census tracts have a median age between 31 and 40 years, indicating a concentration of this age range within the city.

Population Distribution by Age Group

The population distribution across different age groups is as follows:

  • 18-30 years: 25,175 individuals
  • 31-40 years: 39,060 individuals
  • 41-50 years: 151,207 individuals
  • 51-60 years: 131,808 individuals
  • 61+ years: 213,764 individuals

A substantial portion of Portland's population falls within the 61+ years age range, highlighting the dominance of this demographic in the city.

Understanding the Age Distribution

Despite the 61+ years age group being the largest in terms of population size, the median age of the population is younger, specifically between 31 and 40 years. This discrepancy can be attributed to the distribution of the population across various age groups. While the 61+ age group has a significant number of individuals, the combined population of younger age groups (18-30 years, 31-40 years, and 41-50 years) is substantial enough to lower the overall median age. Additionally, the presence of younger families and working-age adults in Portland contributes to a lower median age, reflecting a balanced age distribution rather than a skewed one.
